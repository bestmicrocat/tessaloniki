Amazing things about
Tessaloniki

Each city has its amazing stories and facts.
Tessaloniki is an ancient destination with a very unique vibe covered in legends, myths and stories.
The city has been founded as an important commercial port. Nowadays it is a large meeting point of various culture elements.

The second largest Hellenic city was named after princess Tessalonike, daughter of King Phillip II of Macedon, half-sister of Alexander the Great, also known for the Greek legend about the mermaid who lived in the Aegean. When Alexander the Great died, Tessalonike tried to end her life and turned into a mermaid who kept asking the sailors "Is Alexander the king alive?" If the answer was "He lives and reigns and conquers the world", the mermaid would allow the sailors to sail in calm seas. If the answer was different, the mermaid would transform into a Gorgon who would send the sailors to the bottom of the sea

The patron saint of Tessaloniki was Saint Demetrius, martyred for Christ under the reign of the Emperor Galerius.
In 379 Tessaloniki became the capital of Roman prefecture of Illyricum and its harbor was established then. The 12th century brought an economic expansion to the city, under the rule of the Byzantine emperors.
In 1430 the Ottoman Forces invaded Thessaloniki and ruled the city until the First Balkan war.
During the Second World War the forces of Nazi Germany occupied Tessaloniki. The city suffered damage from bombing but was rebuilt and recovered quickly after the war.
